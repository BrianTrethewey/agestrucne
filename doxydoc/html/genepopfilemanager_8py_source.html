14  '''
15  wraps 2 dictionaries, one for header and loci byte addresses, the other for byte addresses of "pop" lines
16  and individual addresses. The header dict has one level, the pop dict has 3 levels:
17  outer key: for the pop dict, pop number: 1,2..., for header/loci simply the line number
18  middle key: pop dict only, item number: 0,1,2
19  for the pop dictionsary the first "pop" line itself is the zeroth item
20  and for pop 1,2,..., the entry "pop"
21  innermost key: item-line number, 0,1,2...
22  this key needed because genepop allows multi line entries for
23  a single individual
24  value: byte address of the first byte of the line
25 
26  example, if (as most often) the individual entry takes up only one line,
27  to find the entry for the 2nd indiv at pop 2, point the file pointer at
28  the byte address given by self.__byte_addresses[2][2][1], and execute a readline().
29  If this individual was on more than one line, say 2 lines, the entry would 8
30  be given by concatenating the lines read at self.__byte_addresses[2][2][1]
31  and self.__byte_addresses[2][2][2]
32  '''
